Chicken Chilli

Description

Chicken Chilli is a quintessential Indo-Chinese fusion dish that perfectly balances spicy, tangy, and savory flavors. It typically consists of bite-sized pieces of chicken that are battered and deep-fried until golden and crispy, then tossed in a vibrant wok with a thick, glossy sauce. This signature sauce is a high-heat emulsion of soy sauce, green chili paste, vinegar, and sometimes a hint of ketchup, which coats the chicken and creates a mouthwatering glaze that defines the dish.

The texture and aroma of Chicken Chilli are enhanced by the addition of crunchy aromatics, most notably large chunks of sautéed onions and bell peppers. Freshly slit green chilies, minced ginger, and garlic are flash-fried to release their essential oils, providing a sharp heat that cuts through the richness of the fried chicken. Whether served "dry" as a popular appetizer or with a thick gravy alongside fried rice, it remains a celebrated staple of street food stalls and upscale restaurants alike.

Ingredients

  • Boneless chicken cubes
  • Cornflour (Corn starch)
  • All-purpose flour (Maida)
  • Egg
  • Ginger-garlic paste
  • Soy sauce (Dark preferred)
  • Green chilli sauce
  • Vinegar
  • Tomato ketchup
  • Diced onions (large petals)
  • Diced bell peppers (Capsicum)
  • Slit green chillies
  • Minced fresh garlic & ginger
  • Spring onions (chopped for garnish)
  • Cooking oil (for frying)
  • Black pepper, Salt and Water

Steps

  1. Marinate: Mix chicken cubes with one egg, ginger-garlic paste, salt, pepper, and a splash of soy sauce.
  2. Coat: Add cornflour and all-purpose flour to the chicken, tossing until every piece is thickly coated.
  3. Fry: Heat oil and deep-fry the chicken pieces until they are golden brown and crispy, then set aside.
  4. Sauté: In a separate wok with a little oil, flash-fry the minced garlic, ginger, and slit green chillies on high heat.
  5. Vegetables: Add the large onion petals and diced bell peppers; stir-fry for 2 minutes so they stay crunchy.
  6. Sauce: Pour in the soy sauce, green chilli sauce, vinegar, and a tablespoon of tomato ketchup.
  7. Thicken: Mix a teaspoon of cornflour with a little water (slurry) and pour it into the pan to make the sauce glossy.
  8. Toss: Add the fried chicken back into the wok and toss quickly so the sauce coats every piece perfectly.
  9. Garnish: Top with a handful of chopped spring onions and serve immediately while the chicken is still crunchy.
